Pilgrimage has its own vocabulary, and each tradition contributes terms that carry centuries of meaning. This glossary covers the most important terms you'll encounter across the world's major pilgrimage traditions. Entries are grouped by tradition, with cross-tradition terms listed first.

## Cross-Tradition Terms

**Pilgrimage** — a journey to a sacred place undertaken for religious or spiritual purposes. The word derives from the Latin peregrinus, meaning foreigner or stranger. See [what is pilgrimage](/context/what-is-pilgrimage).

**Sacred geography** — the belief that certain physical locations have inherent spiritual significance, forming a landscape of divine presence mapped onto the natural world.

**Relic** — a physical object associated with a saint or holy figure, believed to retain spiritual power. Bones, clothing, and objects of contact are the most common types. See [relics and sacred objects](/context/relics-and-sacred-objects).

**Circumambulation** — the ritual act of walking around a sacred object or site, practiced in Christianity (processions around churches), Islam (tawaf around the Kaaba), Buddhism (kora around Mount Kailash), and Hinduism (pradakshina around temples).

## Christian Terms

**Albergue** — a pilgrim hostel on the Camino de Santiago, offering bunk beds and basic facilities. Municipal albergues are the cheapest; private albergues offer more comfort. See [Camino accommodation](/cities/santiago-de-compostela/where-to-stay-camino).

**Buen Camino** — "good way" in Spanish, the universal greeting and farewell among Camino pilgrims.

**Compostela** — the certificate of completion issued to pilgrims who walk at least the last 100 kilometers to Santiago de Compostela for religious or spiritual reasons.

**Credencial** — the pilgrim passport stamped at stages along the Camino. Required for albergue access and the Compostela certificate. See [Camino etiquette](/cities/santiago-de-compostela/etiquette-camino).

**Hospitalero/a** — the volunteer or staff host at a Camino albergue who manages check-in, welcomes pilgrims, and maintains the facility.

**Indulgence** — in Catholic theology, a reduction of temporal punishment for sin, historically granted for completing certain pilgrimages. The practice was central to medieval pilgrimage economics.

**Stations of the Cross (Via Crucis)** — the fourteen traditional stages of Christ's journey from condemnation to burial, walked by pilgrims along the [Via Dolorosa](/cities/jerusalem/walking-guide-jerusalem-old-city) in Jerusalem and in churches worldwide.

**Via Dolorosa** — "Way of Sorrows," the traditional route of Christ's carrying of the cross through Jerusalem's Old City.

## Islamic Terms

**Hajj** — the annual pilgrimage to [Mecca](/cities/mecca-and-medina), one of the Five Pillars of Islam, obligatory once in a lifetime for every Muslim who is physically and financially able. See [planning guide](/cities/mecca-and-medina/planning-your-hajj-or-umrah).

**Ihram** — the sacred state of purity entered before Hajj or Umrah, marked by specific clothing and behavioral restrictions. See [Hajj etiquette](/cities/mecca-and-medina/etiquette-hajj).

**Tawaf** — the ritual circumambulation of the Kaaba, performed seven times counterclockwise.

**Sa'i** — the ritual walking between the hills of Safa and Marwa, commemorating Hagar's search for water.

**Umrah** — the lesser pilgrimage to Mecca, which can be performed at any time of year. See [Umrah](/routes/umrah).

**Wuquf** — the standing at the Plain of Arafat on the 9th of Dhul Hijjah, the spiritual climax of the Hajj.

**Ziyarat** — the practice of visiting shrines, tombs, and other sacred sites, particularly important in Shia Islam. See [ziyarat in Islam](/context/ziyarat-visiting-shrines-islam).

**Niyyah** — intention, the internal declaration of purpose that precedes Islamic rituals including Hajj and Umrah.

## Jewish Terms

**Aliyah l'regel** — literally "ascending by foot," the biblical obligation to visit the Temple in Jerusalem during the three pilgrim festivals. See [aliyah l'regel](/context/aliyah-leregel).

**Kotel** — the [Western Wall](/places/western-wall), the holiest accessible site in Judaism.

**Kippah (yarmulke)** — a head covering worn by Jewish men at sacred sites and during prayer.

**Kvitel** — a small written prayer or petition placed in the cracks of the Western Wall.

**Shalosh Regalim** — the three pilgrim festivals (Pesach, Shavuot, Sukkot) during which ancient Israelites were commanded to ascend to Jerusalem. See [three pilgrim festivals](/context/three-pilgrim-festivals-judaism).

## Buddhist Terms

**Bodhi Tree** — the fig tree under which the Buddha attained enlightenment at [Bodh Gaya](/places/bodh-gaya), one of Buddhism's [four sacred sites](/context/four-sacred-sites-buddhism).

**Kora** — circumambulation of a sacred site in Tibetan Buddhism, most notably around [Mount Kailash](/routes/kora-mount-kailash).

**Ohenro (お遍路)** — a pilgrim walking the [Shikoku 88 Temple circuit](/routes/shikoku-88-temples) in Japan.

**Shukubō (宿坊)** — temple lodging in Japan, offering tatami rooms, vegetarian meals, and participation in morning prayers. See [Japan accommodation](/cities/kyoto/where-to-stay-kyoto).

**Stupa** — a dome-shaped Buddhist monument containing relics, marking a sacred site, or serving as a focus for meditation.

## Hindu Terms

**Char Dham** — the four sacred abodes at India's cardinal points (Badrinath, Puri, Rameswaram, Dwarka). See [the Char Dham](/context/char-dham-pilgrimage).

**Darshan** — the act of seeing and being seen by a deity in a temple, the primary purpose of Hindu temple pilgrimage.

**Ghat** — a stepped embankment leading to a river, the defining architectural feature of sacred river cities like [Varanasi](/cities/varanasi). See [ghat walking guide](/cities/varanasi/walking-guide-varanasi-ghats).

**Kumbh Mela** — the world's largest pilgrimage gathering, held at four rotating river sites every twelve years. See [the Kumbh Mela](/context/kumbh-mela-gathering).

**Moksha** — liberation from the cycle of death and rebirth (samsara), the ultimate spiritual goal of Hindu pilgrimage.

**Prasad** — food that has been offered to a deity and distributed to worshippers as a blessed gift.

**Tirtha** — a crossing place between the human and divine worlds, the fundamental concept underlying Hindu sacred geography.

**Tirtha-yatra** — pilgrimage, literally "journey to a crossing place."

## Shinto Terms

**Torii (鳥居)** — the distinctive gate marking the entrance to a Shinto shrine, signifying the boundary between ordinary and sacred space.

**Temizuya (手水舎)** — the purification fountain at shrine entrances where visitors wash hands and rinse mouths. See [Japan etiquette](/cities/kyoto/etiquette-japan).

**Ema (絵馬)** — wooden plaques on which shrine visitors write prayers or wishes, hung at designated racks within the shrine grounds.

**Omamori (お守り)** — protective charms sold at shrines and temples, each dedicated to a specific purpose (health, academic success, safe travel, romantic success).
